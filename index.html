Career Tips for New Graduates

Essential advice to help you land your first job and build a successful career

üìù Resume & Portfolio

Highlight Relevant Projects

Include 3-5 projects that demonstrate your technical skills and problem-solving abilities. Use action verbs and quantify your achievements.

Tailor Your Resume

Customize your resume for each job application. Match keywords from the job description to your experience and skills.

Create a Portfolio

Build an online portfolio showcasing your best work. Include project descriptions, code samples, and live demos when possible.

üéØ Job Search Strategy

Use Multiple Platforms

Search on LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor, company websites, and industry-specific job boards. Don't rely on just one platform.

Network Actively

Attend career fairs, join professional associations, and connect with alumni. Many jobs are filled through networking.

Apply Early and Often

Apply to 10-15 jobs per week. The more applications you send, the higher your chances of getting interviews.

üíº Interview Preparation

Research the Company

Learn about the company's mission, recent news, and culture. Prepare thoughtful questions about the role and team.

Practice Technical Questions

Review common technical interview questions for your field. Practice coding problems, system design, or engineering concepts.

Prepare STAR Stories

Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to structure behavioral interview responses with specific examples.

üí∞ Salary Negotiation

Research Market Rates

Use salary data from Glassdoor, PayScale, and industry reports to understand what you're worth in your location and field.

Consider Total Compensation

Look beyond base salary. Consider benefits, stock options, professional development opportunities, and work-life balance.

Practice Your Pitch

Prepare a clear, confident explanation of why you deserve the salary you're requesting. Focus on your value and unique contributions.

üöÄ Career Growth

Continue Learning

Stay updated with industry trends, take online courses, earn certifications, and attend workshops or conferences.

Seek Mentorship

Find mentors in your field who can provide guidance, advice, and help you navigate your career path.

Build Soft Skills

Develop communication, leadership, and teamwork skills. These are often as important as technical abilities for career advancement.

üìä Industry Insights

Follow Industry News

Subscribe to industry publications, follow thought leaders on social media, and join professional groups to stay informed.

Understand Market Trends

Learn about emerging technologies, market demands, and skill requirements in your field to position yourself strategically.

Build Your Brand

Create a professional online presence through LinkedIn, GitHub, or a personal website. Share your knowledge and engage with the community.
